Michiko Dead         By Jack Gilbert

He manages like somebody carrying a box that is too heavy, first with his arms underneath.

When their strength gives out, he moves the hands forward, hooking them on the corners, pulling the weight against his chest.

He moves his thumbs slightly when the fingers begin to tire, and it makes different muscles take over.

Afterward,
he carries it on his shoulder, until the blood
drains out of the arm that is stretched up
to steady the box and the arm goes numb. But now
the man can hold underneath again, so that
he can go on without ever putting the box down.
